What is Lintic?

Lintic is the result of frustration at PDFs as the default way of publishing - and a lack of tools for longform web publishing aimed at the policy/research community.

Lintic takes a Word document (or you can just dump text in) and publishes the content as a nicely formatted webpage. It intelligently creates a navigation structure and uses a DeepCite system to make it so each paragraph can be individually linked and shared.

What's wrong with PDFs?

  • Many are never read. The World Bank found 1/3 of their reports were never even being downloaded. As many again were hardly read at all. A lot of research has already been produced that could go further if it was easier to read/share.
  • A4 in a world of phones. It's hard to read PDFs on mobile devices and these are fast becoming the primary way people access the internet.
  • PDFs cannot be deep-linked. By making it easy to link back to the initial paragraph, we make it more likely people will not only read the interesting information being shared - but that theyâ€™ll read on and learn more from your writing.

PDFs make reading your work harder. We want to make it easier to read and share long content.

Why not just publish on platforms like Wordpress or Medium?

You should do that too! But current platforms are at their best at short lengths and often lack features essential for publishing research. Support for footnotes or charts is usually depending on third-party plugins if available at all.

These platforms will also require you to be comfortable hosting your research on third-party sites or maintaining your own installation. Lintic generates files that you can host yourself with no complicated requirements. The idea is if you can store PDFs, you can store Lintic sites.

PDFs on mobile - ungood if your work benefits from public engagement.

Features:

  • Auto-generates TOC and navigation structure.
  • Can be easily read and shared from mobile and desktops.
  • Automatically creates links for each paragraph - that work even if content is re-arranged.
  • Ability to give readers a 'catchup' when they're linked deep into the document - giving a summary of what you've missed.
  • Smart concealment and reveal of notes and footnotes.
